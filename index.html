Why Kids Love Dinosaur Coloring Pages

Coloring is one of the most beneficial activities for young children. It develops fine motor skills, encourages creativity and self-expression, and helps children learn about colors, patterns, and shapes. When paired with the naturally exciting world of dinosaurs, coloring becomes an educational adventure that sparks curiosity about prehistoric life, paleontology, and natural history.

Our printable pages cover some of the most beloved dinosaur species, giving children the chance to learn each animal's name, characteristics, and habitat while they color:

  • Tyrannosaurus Rex (T-Rex) — the "King of the Dinosaurs," a massive carnivore with tiny arms and a powerful bite
  • Triceratops — a gentle herbivore recognized by its three facial horns and large bony frill
  • Pterodactyl — a flying reptile (technically a pterosaur) that soared over ancient skies
  • Stegosaurus — the plate-backed plant-eater with spikes on its tail called a "thagomizer"
  • Brachiosaurus — one of the tallest dinosaurs, with a long neck perfect for reaching treetops
  • Velociraptor — a fast, intelligent pack hunter with a distinctive curved claw
  • Spinosaurus — a semi-aquatic dinosaur with a dramatic sail-like spine

Tips for Printing Dinosaur Coloring Pages

For the best results, print our coloring pages on standard white 8.5 x 11 inch paper (letter size). All images are high resolution, so they print crisply even on basic inkjet or laser printers. Here are a few tips to get the most out of every page:

  • Set your printer to "Fit to Page" to fill the entire sheet
  • Choose Draft or Economy mode to save ink — the black outlines will still be clear
  • Use cardstock paper if kids will be using markers or watercolors to prevent bleed-through
  • Print multiple copies so kids can try different color combinations
  • Laminate finished pages for a keepsake or display!
