Color By Fun Fact

Use these dinosaur coloring pages to explore the prehistoric world while you color. Each fun fact below comes with a creative prompt — a small challenge that turns a coloring page into a learning moment.

The T-Rex had a bite force stronger than any land animal ever recorded. Fossils and bone analysis suggest Tyrannosaurus Rex could crush with a force of around 8,000 pounds — enough to splinter the bones of its prey whole.

✎ Prompt: Color the teeth as dramatically as possible. Add cracked ground or shaking trees to show the impact of that roar.

Triceratops horns were likely used for more than fighting. Scientists believe the three-horned frill may have also played a role in attracting mates or regulating body temperature, not just in battles with predators.

✎ Prompt: Give your Triceratops a bold, eye-catching frill using warm reds, oranges, and yellows.

Pterodactyls were not dinosaurs — they were flying reptiles. Pterodactylus was a pterosaur that lived alongside dinosaurs but belonged to its own completely separate group. Calling them dinosaurs is one of the most common prehistoric mix-ups.

✎ Prompt: Draw the sky your Pterodactyl is flying through. Stormy and dramatic? Bright and tropical? The background is yours.

Ankylosaurus was essentially a living tank. Its entire back was covered in thick bony plates called osteoderms, and its tail ended in a heavy club it could swing to break the bones of predators like T-Rex.

✎ Prompt: Color each armor plate a slightly different shade to show the texture and depth of that incredible natural armor.

Velociraptors were actually about the size of a turkey. Hollywood made them famous as human-sized predators, but real fossils tell a different story — roughly knee height, feathered, and quick.

✎ Prompt: Draw a familiar everyday object next to your Velociraptor to show just how surprisingly small it really was.

Coloring Page FAQs

Are these dinosaur coloring pages free?

Yes — every single coloring page on this site is completely free to download and print. There is no sign-up, no paywall, and no catch. Click the Download & Print button under any page and it is yours.

Can I use these coloring pages in a classroom or homeschool setting?

Absolutely. All pages are free for personal and educational use, which includes classrooms, homeschool curricula, after-school programs, libraries, and childcare settings. Print as many copies as you need.

What paper is best for printing coloring pages?

Standard white 8.5 x 11 inch printer paper works perfectly for crayons and colored pencils. If your child plans to use markers or watercolors, cardstock paper is a better choice — it is thicker and prevents ink from bleeding through to the other side.

Can I color these pages digitally?

Yes. Download the PNG file and open it in any drawing app that supports image imports. Procreate on iPad, Adobe Fresco, and most other digital art apps work great. Create a new layer on top of the line art and color away without touching the original outlines.

Are any of these coloring pages suitable for toddlers?

Yes — several pages in the collection are specifically designed for very young children. Look for the learning pages (featuring ABC and 123 blocks) and the cute character pages like Dinosaur with Balloon, Dinosaur Waving, and the Group of Baby Dinosaurs. These have large, simple shapes that are easy for small hands to color.

How do I print at the best quality?

Set your printer to Best or High Quality mode and select Fit to Page in the print dialog so the image fills the full sheet. All pages are high resolution, so they will print crisp and clear even on basic home printers. Draft mode also works fine if you want to save ink.
