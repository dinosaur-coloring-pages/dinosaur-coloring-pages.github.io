Color By Fun Fact

Use these dinosaur coloring pages to explore the prehistoric world while you color. Each fun fact below comes with a creative prompt — a small challenge that turns a coloring page into a learning moment.

The T-Rex had a bite force stronger than any land animal ever recorded. Fossils and bone analysis suggest Tyrannosaurus Rex could crush with a force of around 8,000 pounds — enough to splinter the bones of its prey whole.

✎ Prompt: Color the teeth as dramatically as possible. Add cracked ground or shaking trees to show the impact of that roar.

Triceratops horns were likely used for more than fighting. Scientists believe the three-horned frill may have also played a role in attracting mates or regulating body temperature, not just in battles with predators.

✎ Prompt: Give your Triceratops a bold, eye-catching frill using warm reds, oranges, and yellows.

Pterodactyls were not dinosaurs — they were flying reptiles. Pterodactylus was a pterosaur that lived alongside dinosaurs but belonged to its own completely separate group. Calling them dinosaurs is one of the most common prehistoric mix-ups.

✎ Prompt: Draw the sky your Pterodactyl is flying through. Stormy and dramatic? Bright and tropical? The background is yours.

Ankylosaurus was essentially a living tank. Its entire back was covered in thick bony plates called osteoderms, and its tail ended in a heavy club it could swing to break the bones of predators like T-Rex.

✎ Prompt: Color each armor plate a slightly different shade to show the texture and depth of that incredible natural armor.

Velociraptors were actually about the size of a turkey. Hollywood made them famous as human-sized predators, but real fossils tell a different story — roughly knee height, feathered, and quick. The oversized film version was inspired by a larger relative called Deinonychus.

✎ Prompt: Draw a familiar everyday object next to your Velociraptor to show just how surprisingly small it really was.
